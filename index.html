About Me

My first interaction with technology would be the commodore 64 my family got when I was just four years old. I was interested in how the machine worked and played games on it like, Where in the World is Carmen San Diego. Personal computers had caught on by the time I was getting my bachelor's degree from San Francisco State University and I used an orange mac book to complete my assignments.

After college I taught myself how to program and record music on a Triton Korg with a floppy disc drive. I became interested in Graphic Design after creating websites to promote the music I wrote. While pursuing career in music including playing shows in LA and New York, I worked as an Executive Assistant in the high tech, aerospace industry. There I created processes for inventory management, created templates in Corel Draw, helped trouble shoot IT issues, interfaced with vendors and clients, and was the hub of the office. I was the assistant to the CEO Kumar Patel, the inventor of the C02 laser. After working there for four years, I decided it was time to record and produce an album of original electronic music. After putting the album out and working in other part time office jobs in tech, I decided to learn how to code because I was burnt out from office work, and need a career that is creatively fulfilling while continuing to make music and play shows.

I've created websites using CSS, HTML, JS, and Wordpress. I've studied web development on my own and in college courses. I enjoy performing music, listening to bands, biking, technology, writing, art, people watching, and spending time with my husband and two dogs, Akira and Lou.
